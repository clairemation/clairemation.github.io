Padding Your Stats

HTML pages are made up of a collection of elements. The title on the left—that's a header element. This paragraph is a paragraph element.

Actually, that paragraph (and this one too) is a paragraph element inside of a section element. Elements can live inside other elements! This particular section is invisible—roll over the text to see it!

As you can see, elements and their adjustable attributes are a powerful tool for page layout. You can get even more control over the page by using your elements' padding, border, and margin attributes.

Padding, border, and margin all control how much space comes between and element and its surroundings. But what's the difference between them?

At the core of this multilayered treat is the content itself. The border is the line between what's considered "inside" the element and what's "outside."

Padding increases the space between the content and the border—which may make the element grow to accomodate it, if there isn't enough room.

Margin increases the space between the border and the surrounding elements—which may push the element or its neighbor apart.
